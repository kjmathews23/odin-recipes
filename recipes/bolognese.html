Peppery Bolognese

Description

Bolognese is the classic Italian meat sauce making the most out of some simple ingredients, time, and technique. This is a spicy spin on it.

Like Texas hold'em, takes a couple mins to learn and a lifetime to master.

Ingredients

  • 4x cloves of garlic
  • 1x red bell pepper
  • 1x red chili pepper
  • 1x large white onion
  • 4x tbsp olive oil
  • 2x tbsp butter
  • 8oz whole milk
  • 8oz chicken broth
  • 1x bay leaf
  • Your favorite pasta (noodles types are best)
  • 4x sprigs fresh oregano
  • 1lb ground pork
  • 1lb ground beef (85/15 or 90/10 better than 80/20 since the pork has mad fat)
  • 1x can of whole tomatoes (San Marzano or the il whatever)

Steps

  1. Core and de-seed your peppers. Be sure to cut them into bite-sized chunks
  2. Peel and dice the garlic.
  3. Dice your onion. Be sure sure to leave the root attached to avoid tears.
  4. Put your pot on medium low heat, add the olive oil. Once it reached temp, add the onion and peppers. Cook for about 4-5 minutes when onions become milky translucent
  5. Melt the butter in your sofrito, make sure the fire is below medium as you want to melt, not burn, the butter
  6. Saute the garlic for about 45seconds-1min, the garlic should become fragrant
  7. Remove the sofrito from the pan, set aside. Add in your meats and be sure to crumble any large chunks with your wooden spoon. Cook for 8-10 minutes until meat is fully browned. Drain any extra fat
  8. Add in the sofrito from above + your tomatoes. Smash the tomatoes into chunks while stirring.
  9. Add in milk, chicken broth, and bay leafe
  10. Leave on gentle simmer (barely bubbling) on low heat for about 3.5h while stirring. Season w/ salt and pepper to taste. Add in fresh chopped oregano
  11. Serve over noodles that you didn't overcook.
